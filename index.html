Q1a · How Much Each Topic Dominates

What this tells us

  • Over half of all reviews are dominated by lightweight design & aesthetics, the brand's strongest narrative
  • About a quarter discuss quality & structure (space, wheels, handle) broadly positively
  • Nearly a quarter are dominated by talk of broken zips, locks and handles
Red flag: 216 of 924 reviews are primarily about something being broken. That's a material signal, not statistical noise.

Q1b · Implications for Product Management

What features do customers talk about most, and what should the PM do about it?

STRENGTH · PROTECT

Lightweight & aesthetics

Topic 3 covers 51% of reviews and is 96% positive. The lightweight, colourful, easy-to-spot design is a genuine product identity.

Action: Do not touch materials, colour range, or weight profile. Protect in any cost-down programme.
STRENGTH · INVEST

Wheels & interior space

Topic 1 (25% of reviews, 85% positive) centres on 4-wheel spinners, handle, internal compartments. Customers notice these details.

Action: Keep current spec. Consider a minor feature highlight (e.g. named compartments) for the next cycle.
WEAKNESS · FIX NOW

Zips, locks, handles

Topic 2 (23% of reviews, 67% negative). Customers repeatedly report breakages on or within first use.

Action: Urgent supplier & QC audit on zip/lock/handle hardware. Stress-test handle assemblies. This single component cluster is the entire negative narrative.
Bottom line for the product manager: the Bon Air is a well-loved product with one very fixable weakness. Don't redesign what works, just source better zips, locks and handles.

Q2a · What Customers Praise vs. Criticise

Praised aspects

Build quality (good, quality, sturdy), portability (light, lightweight), ease of use (easy, perfect) and aesthetics (nice, excellent). Positive vocabulary is broader and warmer than typical Amazon reviews.

Aspects needing improvement

Breakages (broke: 75, broken: 49), quality shortcuts (poor: 46, faulty: 23, cheap: 22), returns (return: 38, disappointed: 25), surface damage (scratched: 23). Complaints are specific to durability of components, not overall design.

Q2a · Emotional Tone & Sentiment by Topic

Emotional tone (NRC-style)

Joy and trust dominate, customers express affection and brand confidence far more than any negative emotion. Sadness/disgust are present but outweighed roughly 6 : 1.

Sentiment split by topic

The story crystallises here: Topic 3 (Lightweight) is 96% positive, Topic 1 (Quality) is 85% positive. But Topic 2 (Defects) is 67% negative, this is where American Tourister is losing the narrative.

Q2a · At a Glance, Praise vs. Complaint Vocabulary

Praise vocabulary is warm and descriptive, centred on feel (light, easy), appearance (nice, perfect) and endorsement (excellent, sturdy).

Complaint vocabulary is sharp and product-specific, centred on failures (broke, broken, faulty) and consequences (return, disappointed).

Q2b · What This Means for the Marketing Strategy

Five concrete moves for American Tourister's marketing team, each anchored in a specific data finding.

1

Lead with the winning narrative, "Light. Bold. Easy."

96% of Topic 3 reviews are positive. Creative, PDP imagery and social ads should foreground lightweight feel, colour range, wheel smoothness and the detail customers keep noting: "spotting it on the luggage belt". This is organic brand equity ready to be activated.

2

Fix the weakness before scaling spend

Marketing a product with a visible 1-in-5 defect rate will amplify the negative reviews already out there. Any campaign budget should follow a coordinated QC fix on zip/lock/handle hardware.

3

Turn the fix into a marketing moment

Announce the upgrade explicitly, "New reinforced zip & lock system". This directly answers the most common negative keyword cluster, converts doubters into buyers, and resets search-result sentiment over time.

4

Double down on trust, where customers already are

"Trust" is the second-strongest emotion in reviews. Warranty messaging and a clearly visible returns policy convert that emotion into purchase confidence at checkout. Cheap to do, high return.

5

Segment messaging by travel occasion

Travel-context words (holiday, trip, flight, vacation) recur throughout. Tailor creatives for specific use cases, family holiday, short-haul business, long-haul, instead of generic "luggage" positioning.

Conclusion

The Bon Air is a product with a strong heart and a weak link. Three-quarters of customers love what it is, lightweight, well-designed, great-looking. But one cluster of components (zips, locks, handles) is holding back the reputation of the whole line.

For Product

Fix the hardware

Stronger zips, locks, handles. Don't touch anything else. Protect what customers already love.

For Marketing

Lead with the light

Foreground lightness, colour, ease. Announce the quality upgrade. Leverage UGC. Segment by occasion.
